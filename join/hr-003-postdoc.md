### Unit:

Biodiversity Research Center, Academia Sinica

### Job title:

Postdoctoral Researcher

### Work content:

Our research team is seeking a Postdoctoral Researcher to join us in
studying the ecology and evolution of plant–microbe symbiosis. We
integrate field ecology, molecular microbiology, and genomic analyses to
investigate how microbial communities assemble and adapt across hosts
and environments. This position is open until filled.

Current research topics:

-   Host identity on symbiont community assembly

-   Ecological genomics of symbionts across native plant hosts

Job description

-   Conduct field sampling, mostly in roadside, urban, and agricultural
    environments

-   Lead and participate in research projects

-   Design and conduct experiments and data analyses

-   Actively contribute to scientific writing and oral presentations

-   Mentor junior researchers and assist in training activities

-   Participate in routine laboratory tasks and other assigned duties

### Qualifications: 

-   PhD in ecology, evolution, genomics, bioinformatics, microbiology,
    plant science, or a related field

-    Strong skills in one or more of the following areas: (1) Fieldwork
    and ecological sampling; (2) Programming (R, Python, or others); (3)
    Benchworks (molecular or microbiological experiments); (4)
    Mathematical modeling (theory or simulations)

-   Ability to lead independent research projects and collaborate
    effectively within a team

-   Self-motivated, detail-oriented, and well-organized, with strong
    communication skills

-   Proficiency in professional English writing and oral communication

-   Experience in scientific publishing and conference presentations

Our institute is part of Academia Sinica, the Taiwanese academy of
sciences. The working language in our lab and institute is English.
Knowledge in Mandarin or other Taiwanese languages is not required, but
foreign researchers are encouraged to take Mandarin classes if they'd
like to. Inquiries are also welcome.
